Automation, Robotics and Computer Vision (AUROVA) Group is a research team of University of Alicante (UA). The group is dedicated to exploring intelligent techniques applied to robotic manipulation and autonomous robots. Our research encompasses a wide range of topics including, but not limited to:

Intelligent robotic manipulation.
  • Detection of the grasp points with vision techniques.
  • Analysis of the grasp stability and slip detection.
  • Grasping and manipulation based on Reinforcement Learning.
  • Recognition with tactile sensors.
Autonomous mobile robots.
  • Navigation in outdoor environments.
  • Navigation in dynamic and unstructured environments.
  • Localization based on geo-referrenced maps.
  • LiDAR odometry.
  • Human-robot interaction.
